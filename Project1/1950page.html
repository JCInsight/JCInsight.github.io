Paul Anka
Before the rise of pop and hiphop, there was Paul Anka. He was a personal favourite of mine - not because he was a fellow Canadian, but because his music had a certain charm that actually made me a fan. Even to this day, I stil am a fan of his music. His most popular songs were actually my favourite songs at the time (about 1957-1959), like "Put Your Head on my Shoulder", "Diana", and others which really resonated with me at the time.

Television

Television was something that for me, came around about the year '57? '58? In retrospect, it was a radio with moving pictures and a bigger box. However, it did change how we viewed everyday life; Families and friends would huddle around television sets instead of radios, and they would then watch and listen instead of the latter. Every single person in the neighbourhood had one of these, but there were hardly any relatable shows for a teenager like me. The screens were of black and white, but having something to watch already felt like a fever dream.
